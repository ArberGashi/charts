# ArberCharts Service Level Agreement (SLA)

This Service Level Agreement (SLA) defines response targets for ArberCharts support requests. The SLA applies to customers with an active paid support subscription. All response times are measured during Business Hours unless stated otherwise.

## 1. Business Hours & Time Zone

- **Business Hours**: Monday–Friday, 09:00–18:00 CET/CEST  
- **Response time**: Time until first qualified response  
- **Resolution time**: Target for fix, workaround, or action plan

## 2. Severity Levels

### P1 — Critical
System is unusable in production, core functionality is blocked, or critical security issue.

### P2 — High
Major functionality is degraded, no viable workaround, significant business impact.

### P3 — Medium
Non-critical defect or performance issue with a workaround available.

### P4 — Low
Minor issue, documentation request, enhancement suggestion.

## 3. Response & Resolution Targets

| Severity | Response (Enterprise) | Response (Professional) | Resolution Target |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| P1 | 4 business hours | 1 business day | Hotfix within 3–5 business days |
| P2 | 8 business hours | 2 business days | Patch in next release |
| P3 | 2 business days | 3 business days | Scheduled release |
| P4 | 5 business days | 5 business days | Scheduled release |

## 4. Support Scope

Included:
- Core framework defects
- Renderer correctness or performance regressions
- Integration guidance for supported configurations

Excluded:
- Customer-specific customizations
- Third-party library issues
- Changes outside documented API contracts

## 5. Customer Responsibilities

To ensure fast resolution, please provide:
- Repro steps and minimal sample data
- Stack traces/logs
- JDK version and OS details
- Renderer and dataset type

## 6. Updates & Hotfix Policy

- P1 issues receive a prioritized hotfix.
- P2 issues are addressed in the next patch release.
- P3/P4 issues are planned based on roadmap and impact.

## 7. SLA Credits (Optional)

At ArberCharts' discretion, repeated SLA violations may be compensated with service credits on renewal.
